A Whole-Body Approach

Your Mouth Is a Window Into the Rest of You

Most dental visits don’t start with a question about your sleep, your gut, or your energy. They probably should. What’s happening at the gum line, in the oral microbiome, or around an older restoration often reflects something larger in the body.

Holistic dentistry isn’t about rejecting conventional care. It’s about asking better questions before reaching for the standard answer — and looking for the root cause so the same problems stop coming back.

At Sunset Biological Dentistry, Dr. Mersedeh Rajinia considers how your oral health connects to your airway, your immune system, and your long-term wellbeing. The goal has always been the same: support your body’s own ability to heal.

Materials & Methods

Biocompatible Materials, Gentler Tools

The materials placed in your mouth stay with you for years, so it’s worth choosing them carefully. Dr. Mersedeh works with metal-free, biocompatible restorations that bond to the tooth and preserve as much healthy structure as possible.

Biological tools make visits more precise and often gentler — ozone to address bacteria without harsh chemicals, PRF (platelet-rich fibrin made from your own blood) to support healing after procedures, and 3D imaging for a clearer, more complete picture.

Some of these approaches rest on well-established evidence and others are still emerging. We’ll always tell you which is which, so you can make a decision grounded in fact rather than hype.

On Your Terms

You Stay in Control of Every Decision

You’ll never be pushed toward treatment you don’t need. Dr. Mersedeh takes the time to walk you through what she finds, in plain language you can actually use, and then the next step is yours to make.

This isn’t fear-based. It’s awareness. Understanding what’s happening in your mouth — and how it connects to the rest of your health — lets you choose with clarity instead of pressure.

For patients who feel conventional dentistry has missed the bigger picture, that difference is the whole point: care that treats you as a whole person, at your pace.
