What It Is

Ozone Sounds High-Tech. It’s Really Just Activated Oxygen.

Ozone is simply oxygen in an activated form — think of it as oxygen carrying a little extra energy. In dentistry, medical-grade ozone has been used for many years as a natural way to disinfect.

We can deliver it as a gas, as ozonated water, or in an oil, depending on what the situation calls for. Because it works with your body rather than against it, ozone has become one of the most useful tools in biological dentistry.

It lets us care for your mouth thoughtfully, with fewer chemicals along the way.

How We Use It

Usually the Quiet Step That Makes Everything Else Better

Ozone is rarely the whole treatment. More often it’s the step that makes the rest of your care gentler and more thorough.

We use it to disinfect a tooth before placing a restoration, supporting a cleaner foundation. Ozonated water can flush gum pockets to reduce the bacteria associated with inflammation and bleeding. After an extraction or surgery, ozone can support your body’s natural recovery. And in the early stages of decay, it can reduce bacteria as part of a remineralization approach.

One tool, many gentle applications.

Why It Fits

Antibiotic-Free, Evidence-Informed, Patient-First

Ozone has strong antimicrobial evidence behind it, which is part of why we rely on it so often. It allows us to reduce bacteria without reaching first for antibiotics or harsh agents.

It isn’t a cure-all, and we won’t present it as one. What it is: a well-supported way to make care more comfortable and more biological — designed to support healing, never to override it.
